AMY'S FAVORITE RECIPE

The Best Lentil Salad, Ever (Really!)

Although lentils can be boring, this salad is a real crowd-pleaser, with a sweet-savory dressing that's surprisingly addictive. It's great for summer potlucks, lunches, and dinners, and it works well if you like to batch cook since it will keep for several days in the refrigerator.

Special Note: Be sure to use French du Puy lentils. These small, black lentils will have a better texture and firmness than regular green or brown lentils, which will become too mushy.

The recipe is from Sarah Britton's fantastic blog, My New Roots; her cookbook, by the same name, also has many tasty recipes.

INGREDIENTS:

Lentil Mixture

  • 2 1/4 cups (1 lb.) Du Puy lentils
  • 1 medium red onion, diced
  • 1 cup dried currants or raisins, chopped finely
  • 1/3 cup capers

Vinaigrette

  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp. maple syrup
  • 1 Tbsp. strong mustard (I like Grey Poupon)
  • 2 tsp. salt
  • 2 tsp. pepper
  • 1 tsp. ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp. turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp. ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp. ground cardamom
  • 1/4 tsp. cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 tsp. ground cloves
  • 1/4 tsp. ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp. ground cinnamon

Optional Add-Ins

  • Arugula
  • Walnuts
  • Goat cheese
  • Fresh herbs (parsley, cilantro, basil)
  • Sprouts
  • Crispy seasonal vegetables

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Rinse lentils well and drain in colander. Place in a pot and cover with 3-4 inches of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to simmer. You will know they are cooked if they still retain a slight tooth or chewiness (al dente). Overcooking the lentils is the death of this dish. Be careful!

  2. While the lentils are simmering, make the vinaigrette by placing all ingredients in a jar with a tight fitting lid and shaking vigorously to combine.

  3. Finely dice the red onion - the salad is best if all the ingredients are about the same size. If using raisins, chop them roughly to make them a bit smaller, and do the same with the capers if they are large.

  4. When the lentils are cooked, remove from heat, drain, and place under cold running water to stop the cooking process. Once cooled slightly but still a little warm, place the lentils in a large serving bowl and toss with the vinaigrette. Add onion, capers, and raisins. If using other add-ins such as herbs, greens, or cheese, wait until just before serving. Otherwise, this salad can hang out in the fridge for a couple of days.
